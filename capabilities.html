Principles

How we think about data.

Opinions earned from engagements, not from textbooks.

01

Show the variance, not the zero.

Occupancy doesn't live between 0% and 100% — it lives between 85% and 95%. If the difference between a great month and a bad month doesn't show up on the chart, the chart isn't doing its job. Axes get scaled to where the business actually operates.

02

Dashboards read only from deliberate tables.

Nothing in production queries intermediate models. Every dashboard sources from a dashboard-layer table — a deliberate, reviewed transformation. This keeps surprise breakages out of client views when upstream data shifts.

03

If a human does it twice, automate it.

Sending the same follow-up email. Checking the same report each morning. Reconciling the same two spreadsheets. The second time through is the signal. The third time through is embarrassing.

04

The stack is the deliverable.

No lock-in, no black box. Your GCP project. Your GitHub repo. Your Metabase instance. If we vanished tomorrow, the system keeps running — and any competent engineer can pick it up.

05

A human is always on the other end.

AI helps us build and maintain faster than a team our size has any business doing. But it doesn't replace us. When you send a question, a person reads it. When a change ships, a person reviewed it. You are not being handed off to a model.
